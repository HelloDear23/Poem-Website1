Sharp Teeth

  • Back to Home
  • Previous Poem

I have a joy that is hungry.
It gnaws at my chest cavity with malignant glee,
Forcing deranged giggles from my throat.
I feel my grin bare its ugly teeth.

I try to hide this hunger.
Tuck my pointed teeth under my lip,
Making sure that I have the right number of teeth.

Yet I know that the hunger will always show.
It will be by the glint in my eye,
A red tint that pierces like a knife.

The hunger is a part of me.
I have starved so long for brightness,
That it becomes dark when I finally grasp it.
My joy is starving.
