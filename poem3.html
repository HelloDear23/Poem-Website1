Turning

There’s that running gag in media—
“Oh my god, I’m turning into my parents!”
It’s a trope that gets a moment-thought, easy chuckle,
But can give ya’ a night-long, scared spiral.

    My dad was always afraid I’d turn into him.
I’d slip comfortably into his too-smart, too-cool lackadaisical attitude,
And wind up wearing it around town,
        around down
          to nowhere.
But instead, his person fit me like tight pair of shoes,
Pinching the feet of my determination and sense of right-and-wrong.

Nope—no sir—I am not wearing the skin of my father!

    But.
        I am afraid I will end up like my mother.
That her sweet, naïve person will fit me like a long lost coat,
Her battered, smiling face will be just in my color tone.
