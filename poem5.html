Black powder, spreads thin
Painting teens, like volto masks.
Stripes cover arms, bunched like rope
Hiding scars, like Venetian gowns.

Life is a Carnival, filled with lights and lies.
It tells us not to talk too openly,
Behind closed doors, drawn drapes, or down domes.
Loose lips are left all alone.

Like a broken lamp, you are too sharp to touch.
Like a feral cat, you hiss and claw at all who come near.
Like a winter wind, you turn others’ fingers frozen solid.
Like a drowned dog, you sink deeper into the blue.

Why do you cry, o’ veiled one?
Why wear your hood so high?
Why do you paint your arms red, young one?
Don’t you have more life to live?
