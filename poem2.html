Latch-Key Kids

  • Next Poem

Do you ever wonder
    if we’re all just latch-key kids?
We’re exploring the world like 13 year olds;
    full of angst and wonder and fear.
We have the key to the Heavenly gates—
    Our absentee Parent wouldn’t leave us locked out—
And yet.

    Isn’t it more fun?
Roaming the sidewalks, the arcades, and gardens;
    Getting into trouble with friends?
We do not fill our bellies with home food,
    Just with hot pockets and candy, and things we know
They wouldn’t let us have when They’re home.

    Sure— it’s lonely.
Waiting for Them to come home.
    Our prayers and hymns like voice messages left to a busy office phone;
Our breaths are always held for a reply.

Maybe some of us lose our keys.
    Fall too far into the fun,
leaving what gets us home in a bush, or a pipe, or another’s bed.

    Maybe we’re all just latch-key kids.
And maybe They’re too busy working.
    But at least we’re all in the same neighborhood.
