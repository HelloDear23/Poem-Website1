All the Cabinets are Open

  • Back to Home
  • Next Poem

I think my house is haunted.
I swear, there’s a spirit there,
Or maybe I just can’t recall.

I go into rooms and things aren’t how I remember,
My clothes are on my bed instead of in my closet,
My dishes are half-put, half-left,
And all my cabinets are open.
Or maybe I just can’t recall.

I think it possesses me too.
It sucks my energy from me, or fills me with the brim to it.
It stills my hand and freezes my heart, with my head a helpless onlooker.
How can I do these tasks, these daily chores and deadlines,
When I am haunted?
Or maybe I just can’t recall

I’ve sought professionals.
They salt and burned my old bed— “the covers are holding you back!”
They salt and burned my food— “the sugars are too high!”
They salt and burned my everything until they just say,
“It’s you”
“You are too lazy, too distracted,
you just gotta try,
you aren’t trying,
TRY HARDER”.
Or maybe I just can’t recall.

Finally a lady with a slow drawl and a Picasso in her waiting room found my ghost.
It wasn’t with EVPS, SLS, spirit boxes, or dousing rods,
But with a list and a book.
“How often you find yourself interrupting people?”
“How often do you make careless mistakes when doing a boring task?”
And every, “How often,” pulled my spirit from its grave to stare me down.

It's ADHD, if I recall.
