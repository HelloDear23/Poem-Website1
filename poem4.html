As Whitman looked at the sea, birds chirping all about,
And Duncan looked at streams of words, aching and wanting,
I looked into the shadows of streetlamps.


The darkness could be a friend.
I found that through focused fear, looking from my bedroom.
It conceals bruises, silences footsteps, and quiets the night.


Light was the enemy–
When morning came all would reset–
Cars would drive, silence would break, and school would start


No, night needed nothing but your thoughts.
Your brain could beam images in the darkness, with a world better than before.
Apologies would be said, fathers would leave, and children would wail.


Daydreams are wispy at best–
Fragile like glass figurines–
Words would be forgotten, ideas would be flying, and mental grasp would be weak


The yellow light makes the black bolden.
The shapes become forms and couplets.
Tears would be metaphors, drinks would be page breaks, and screams would be rhymes.


As the sea whispered me,
And kingdom or salvation or freedom is never known,
The shadows of streetlamps offer lullabies to a tortured soul.
